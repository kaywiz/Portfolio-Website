Urban Chapel

For the first portion of Arch 211, we studied properties of light and methods of light manipulation. For the rest of the semester, we translated these studies, along with observations from the Concordia Seminary Park site, into the design of an urban chapel.

Light Manipulation

While I began with an asymmetric series of modules, I moved towards a gridded system of differently tapered apertures set in locations specific to the path of the sun across the sky. The tapering created negative space underneath the model, as well a way to control the condition of light created below the apertures.

HCW create a static light manipulation device that captures the sun's dynamic path across the sky?

Site Observations

HCW determine the ideal location of an urban chapel within the park according to current concentrations of activity?

My site observations in the Concordia Sanctuary Park focused on the path, demographic, and activity level of pedestrians over the span of two weeks.

Urban Chapel

HCW design an urban chapel that builds off the studies of light and site observations and provides a private worship space and a reflective public space that draws pedestrians away from the centers of commerce at the perimeter of the site?
