Flying Machine

We began Arch 112 by selecting a flying machine precedent and studying its principles of flight. Thoughout the semester, we continued to study aerodynamics through a series of prototypes, with the ultimate goal of building a flying machine of our own design.

Precedent

I chose a specialized cellular kite as my precedent. I was intrigued by the jerky rotation of the kite as it flew; my precedent was attempting to adapt to the changing wind pressure, but the construction of the kite forced it to rotate as a unit, therefore inhibiting its ability to fly as efficiently as possible.

Prototype 1

HCW translate this idea of rotation into a prototype that allows for more efficient, individual rotation of sails?

This prototype allowed me to study the effects of relative alignment and scale on the movement of the sails.

Prototype 2

HCW improve on the previous prototype to allow it to catch more wind from multiple directions?

This prototype maintained the freedom of movements of individual sails, but connected them to bring more cohesion. The sails are now made from a sturdier and more permeable woven plastic, and they take on a more parafoil shape to catch more wind.

However, the large areas of overlap between the layers of sails added weight that wasn't benefitting the prototype.

Flying Machine

HCW maintain the element of individually-rotating but connected sails while allowing them to adjust mid-air to capture as much air and create as much lift as possible?

As the sails catch air, they rotate, creating centripetal force. This pushes each of the 5 fins in the different layers to spread out a certain distance to minimize overlap and maximize surface area and lift.
